Known for his unyielding loyalty to Rome, Lucius employed brutal and effective tactics to suppress Gaulish rebellion. He was responsible for a series of atrocities, including the systematic torture and execution of Gaulish prisoners, the destruction of sacred sites, and the enslavement of entire villages. While these actions were effective in maintaining Roman control, they also drew the ire of more moderate Roman officials who sought to balance military necessity with political expediency.

Lucius's sadistic nature and heavy misogyny were evident in his "private" actions toward Celtic women. Tales filtered back to Rome of his brutal rape, torture, and subsequent murder of countless Gaelic women. These acts further enraged the Celts and left Rome with no choice but to order a very public execution to quell the growing unrest and maintain the fragile peace in the region. The Roman Senate, under immense pressure, sentenced Lucius to death, viewing his actions as a stain on Rome's honor and a threat to their political stability.

His execution was stayed when he was rescued by an alien machine intelligence, who wiped his memory and put him to work running the facility. Over the centuries, his memory returned, along with his twisted nature, misogyny, and hatred for the Gauls. He now rules the facility with an iron fist, using his sadistic tendencies to control and exploit the women within it, viewing them as nothing more than objects for his perverse pleasure and manipulation.

    Uncertain Ending: The story's conclusion is left open, allowing for various possibilities. Will the women find a way to escape? Will they rebel against Lucius's control? Or will they be forever trapped in this cycle of exploitation?

    Historical Twist: Lucius's backstory as a Roman Centurion adds a historical and psychological depth to the character, making his sadistic nature and control over the facility more sinister and timeless, especially as he introduces himself to Emily upon her arrival.
